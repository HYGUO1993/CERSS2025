LSTM-Augmented Probability-Informed Neural Network for Efficient Time-Dependent Reliability Analysis

15:45

Dr. Hongyuan Guo (PolyU)

▶ View Abstract
This study proposes a novel LSTM-augmented Probability-Informed Neural Network Evolution (LPNE) method. The framework integrates the temporal sequence learning capabilities of LSTM networks with the physical constraints of the Generalized Density Evolution Equation (GDEE).

From Climate Change to Urban Resilience: A Multi-scale Assessment of Future Typhoon Wind Hazards

16:00

Dr. Jiayao Wang (Notre Dame / PolyU)

▶ View Abstract
This research develops a multi-scale framework to assess how climate change influences typhoon wind hazards, incorporating projected changes in key indicators (e.g., SST) and dynamically downscaling typhoon scenarios using the WRF model.

Risk Assessment and Resilience Improvement of Power Grids under Typhoon-Related Compound Hazards

16:15

Meng Yang (PolyU)

▶ View Abstract
This study focuses on the risk assessment and resilience enhancement strategies for power grids under typhoon–heatwave compound disasters. A high-fidelity physical simulation model for tropical cyclones is developed.

Sequential Overflow Risk Assessment Framework for Storm Surge with Climate Change

16:30

Qikun Wei (PolyU)

▶ View Abstract
This study develops a risk assessment framework to provide consistent insights from an integrated time-spatial perspective for storm surges in Hong Kong between 2030 and 2080.

Vulnerability and Risk Assessment of Coastal Bridges under Extreme Wave and Wind Hazards

16:45

Deming Zhu (PolyU)

▶ View Abstract
This research presents a comprehensive vulnerability and risk analysis framework for coastal bridges. To efficiently estimate wave-induced loads, a surrogate modeling approach based on LSTM neural networks is developed.

High-order moments-based iterative maximum entropy method (HOM-IMEM) for global reliability assessment of structures
